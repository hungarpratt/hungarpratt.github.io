The Costs of Dog Ownership

What Did We Get Ourselves Into?!

Though not as expensive as raising a child or purchasing a home, dog ownership is a MAJOR financial commitment. When we conceded to Eva's wish for a Covid dog, we knew it would entail some expenditures. Little did we know! expenses.

Teaching Responsibility

Initially, we had Eva manage the spreadsheet for all dog-related expenses. Here is her initial list of categories:

  1. food
  2. dog bed
  3. toys
    • plush toys
    • chew toys
    • squeeky toys
    • eduational toys
    • toys to sleep with

While we appreciated Eva's focus on the toy department, we explained that she should consider expanding the overall list to encompass other necessities such as visits to the vet. We helped her flesh out the budget.

The Money Pit

Particularly at the beginning, costs mounted up quickly - training, vaccinations, a crate, tons of toys (of course!) and -- a real unexpected treat - eye surgery!! After 6 months, we told Eva she could stop actively maintaining the spreadsheet; we preferred to remain ignorant rather than being constantly reminded of the mounting costs.

The annual costs have stabilized, but they are still significant. Based on my credit card statements from 2023, I have pieced together the following annual budget. I'm sure this underestimates the true costs (i.e., would we have replaced the sofa if Riley hadn't tracked mud all over it?), but it's a pretty good approximation.
