The Life of Riley

In the Beginning...

It's a typical family story. From the time the kids were little they begged for a dog. We were subjected to heartfelt pleas afor years. As they got older, they resorted to more creative approaches:

  1. Power Point presentations
  2. Multi-page essays
  3. Live testimonials from friends
  4. Incessant whining

Finally, when our oldest was about 12 and our youngest 8, we decided to bite the bullet and get a dog. However, the gods intervened. We learned that our oldest daughter Maya was allergic (even to so-called hypo allergenic dogs). The project came to a grinding halt.

Never Say Never!

Fast forward many years... September 2020, several months into the pandemic. The older two kids were out of the house, and our youngest was at home by herself doing virtual school. She renewed her pleas for a dog. We reminded her of her older sister's allergy. In response, she made the case that Maya was "grown up" and off on her own. But what about when she visits home, which we hoped would be often? Ultimately, the question boiled down to this: Would the benefits of providing Eva companionship during Covid outweigh the aggravation of Maya taking Zyrtec every time she visited?

We told Eva that she needed to work it out with Maya. Remarkably, she convinced Maya with a simple promise: she'd give her older sister naming rights. Maya chose the name Chestnut, based on the species' coloring and the fact that we live on Chestnut Street.

However, in a characteristically Machiavellian move, Eva decided that Chestnut was the formal name -- but her "nickname" would be Riley (by "nickname", Eva meant the one we'd be using 99.999% of the time).

What's in a Name?

As they say, man plans and God laughs. Maya was not supposed to return until the summer, but she decided to come back to NJ and work for half a year before college. On the afternoon of her return, Maya bounded into the home to meet her new dog, Chestnut. Needless to say, the dog did not respond to the name. That's when Eva explained the situation. Sneaky little sister strikes again!
